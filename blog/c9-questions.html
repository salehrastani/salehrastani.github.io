How to Ask the Right Question

Good Questions

January 11, 2015

One of the most important things in learning, is asking the right question. What does that exactly mean? Well, it is more of a process that mostly takes place before the question itself. The first thing that a learner has to do is to try to come across the answer on his own. Checking the internet, or reviewing your notes are a few ways to find your answer before you've asked it. The chances are that the same question has been asked by someone else before, and it can be found on the web.

Once your sure that you cant find what you want yourself and you have to ask, the question has to be clear. The most important thing is to ask a question that is to the point and precise. Asking general questions like: "What is .each in ruby?" is not a good idea if your simply looking for a difference between .each and .map . The more specific the question the better. Adding examples is always helpful.
