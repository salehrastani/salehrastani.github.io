Pairing and Feedback

November 29, 2014

Pairing with another developer who is trying to learn can be challenge fore all of us, but it definitely presents great benefits that would not be reached otherwise. My first pairing was one of the most enlightening ones. Breaking the ice and jumping into something to work on with another person is just spontaneously spectacular. I realized that my biggest fear was to go too slow or too fast in the pairing session. Ultimately, what I feared the most was what I was best at. It was eyeopening. Not only did I learn a lot about pairing but I learned a lot about myself.

Pairing gave me the vonfidence to work with other developers and allowed me to build a structure to how I wanted my pairing sessions to proceed. However, a frustrating aspect of pairing was the constant challenge of expressing yourself and refraining from over-expressing. It is very important to give the pair the time to focus and respond adequetely before tossing out another option or route or opinion.

When it comes to giving and receiving feedback I feel like the most important thing is to take the feedback with a grain of salt since words arent always interpreted the same way they are intended. Apart from that, feedback was for sure a key element to my learning. Although, pairing brings out the best and worse of me and makes me realize what I need to do to be a good pair, feedback is the ultimate teacher. My pairs were very kind in giving me actionable and specific feedback on the challenges we faced and overcame.

Overall I've loved the process of learning and teaching in the form of pairing and feedback. It really doesn't matter how smart I or anybody else might be, my pair can definitely steer me clear of things that I am unaware of. There's always things that you know that another doesn't.
