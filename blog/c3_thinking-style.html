The Growth Mindset

DBC Thinking Style

November 14, 2014

When it comes to learning I am the type of person that has to learn everything. Having a comprehensive outlook on what I am doing is of great consequence to me. If I am doing a project that entails Ruby arrays, I will need to learn everything there is about Ruby arrays that might come up. I am not saying that I actually will learn everything all Im saying is that Ill try and learn everything. If Im learning about arrays on the web and I see a new method being used I will have to stop myself from searching that method as well.

I feel like this way of learning has impacted my learning drastically, in a bad way that is. Devbootcamp has given me the both the motivation and the thinking style to overcome this trait. The motivation comes from the fact that I donâ€™t have time to learn everything. There are deadlines that I need to abide by. If the material nad challenges are not done by the deadline Ill have to face the consequences. I have obviously paid a significant amount of money and only have a few months to make or break, so Im going to make it.

Reading the cultural material that DBC initially required, I cam to the realization that one of the main reasons for my perfectionism was the fear of being wrong. The culture and mindset that DBC has promoted is the growth mindset, where one feels that learning is part of their entity. The growth mindset advocates trying, and with trying being wrong. Whether it is a pairing session with a peer or a guided pairing session an instructor the only thing that matters is to try and learn. It truly does not matter what you previously knew, what you might remember and how quick you pick up things. It is merely about being in a moment and investing yourself completely to the task at hand.

The only thing I personally need to do is to continue on with this behavior until the attitude becomes permanent. There definitely will be challenges to come in the future but if I can endure the mental pain and persist I will surely thrive.
