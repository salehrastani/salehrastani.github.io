Stereotype Threat

Stereotype Threat

December 8, 2014

I wish I could say that I have never felt a stereotype threat my whole life. Unfortunately, that's not the case. Growing up in a Iranian family that gave enormous weight to academic acheivement and degrees I was always compeled to struggle to become an attorney or doctor. I knew that I didnt want to be a doctor cause it was simply too much, so I shot for law school and went down the same path most graduates from college take when they aren't sure what to do.

Although I knew what I was doing, I didn't allow myself to express my own thoughts and feelings simply because I was under the impression that I should not deviate from what I was brought up to understand as my responsibilities and the culturally pre-set scenario of scoring a prestigeous job.

Law school was not easy, but I went through with it. However, throughout the entire time that I was grinding myself in all those classes and getting closer and closer to becoming an attorney, I would ask myself whether I was doing the wrong thing by listening to the presumptions I had about what I was supposed to achieve in life.

I ultimately decided not to become a lawyer and turned to software development because law was the most boring thing I had ever imagined doing. It wasn't an easy decision. I had invested a lot of time, money and effort in law school, but I had reached my threshold and was fet up with all of it.And so upon the conclusion of law school, I decided not to practice law and started a journey in the "Hello World!" world.
