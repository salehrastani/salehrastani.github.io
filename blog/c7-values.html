Who Am I and What Do I Care About?

Self-Affirmation

December 14, 2014

These are the values that are most important to me as a human being:

Excellence
Insight
Happiness
Compassion
Tranquility

For the most part I try my best to stay honest to myself when it comes to acquiring and attaining these qualities. They are the few pillars in my life that dictate whether I am on the right path or not. Apart from what happens outside of my mind, the only thing that will bring me down in my opinion is the mentality that I have. Paying attention and reviewing these values that I hold so dearly reminds me what I want in life, what I am looking to acheive, and what is truly important to me aside from all the stereotype commosion that is happening outside of me.

A person is definitely defined by what he/she does, not what he/she is percieved to do. Throughout my life I have constantly witnessed excellence happen around me and realized that almost all the people who have acheived were less fortunate than me when it came to having the world on their side. The truth of the matter is that those people are in fact, successful at whatever is important for them, regardless of what stereotypes were against them.

The mind is easily susceptible to what others say about us. However, it can also be altered by realizing what is truly important to us. If we can constantly remind ourselves who we truly are by remembering what is most important to us we can overcome the adversities in our way.
